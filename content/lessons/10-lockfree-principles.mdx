## What this lesson will cover

- The progress hierarchy: obstruction-free ⊂ lock-free ⊂ wait-free.
- Linearizability: a history has an equivalent sequential history consistent with real-time order.
- Why lock-free is *not* the same as faster. When blocking locks win.
- Helping: wait-free algorithms typically require threads to help each other finish.
- Universal construction (Herlihy): anything sequentially specifiable is wait-free-implementable (at a cost).
