## What this lesson will cover

- x86-TSO (Total Store Ordering): stores are ordered; only store→load reordering is allowed (via the store buffer).
- ARM (AArch64) weak ordering: any reorder allowed unless a barrier forbids it.
- POWER: even weaker — address dependencies don't always order loads.
- Why `smp_mb()`, `dmb ish`, `hwsync` exist and where C11 atomics emit them.
- IRIW (Independent Reads of Independent Writes) as the defining test.
