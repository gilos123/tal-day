## What this lesson will cover

- Two CAS loops: enqueue swings `tail`, dequeue swings `head`.
- The dummy node: why the queue is never truly empty.
- Helping: if tail is lagging (points to a node whose `next` is non-NULL), the
  enqueuer must advance it before completing its own work.
- Memory reclamation implications (still lesson 15).
- Why a straightforward "swap the node" approach doesn't work.
