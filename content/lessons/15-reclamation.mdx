## What this lesson will cover

- The fundamental problem: a retired node may still be referenced by any thread
  that had snapshotted it before retirement.
- Hazard pointers (Michael 2004): O(1) per-operation space, O(N×M) scan on retire.
- Epoch-based reclamation (EBR): global epoch counter, free nodes two epochs old.
- Quiescent-state reclamation: nodes free only at points threads promise not to hold any.
- Trade-offs: hazard pointers bound memory tightly; EBR is faster in the common case.
